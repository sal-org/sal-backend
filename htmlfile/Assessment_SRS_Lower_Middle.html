Factors that Contribute to Resiliency

Build your connections.Prioritize relationships. A strong support system of family and friends can boost our resilience in the most challenging times. Along with one-on-one relationships, join a group. Some people find that being active in civic groups, faith-based communities, or other local organizations provides social support and can help you reclaim hope.

Foster wellness.Promoting a positive lifestyle to include proper nutrition, sleep, hydration, regular exercise, mindful journaling, yoga, meditation, and other practices can help when we are dealing with situations that require resilience.

Find purpose.Be proactive. If the problems seem too big to tackle, break them down into manageable pieces. Develop some realistic goals even if it seems like a small accomplishment — that enables you to move toward the things you want to accomplish.

Embrace healthy thoughts.Accept that change is a part of life. An optimistic outlook empowers you to expect that good things will happen to you. Remind yourself of where you’ve been able to find strength and ask yourself what you’ve learned from those experiences. Avoid negative outlets.

Seek Help.If negative thoughts overwhelm your ability to make positive changes, these are occasions to seek out a professional to assist us. Many benefits can result from finding a therapist, counsellor, or other to help at times. Take appointment today with your SAL therapist because your health and life are worth it.
